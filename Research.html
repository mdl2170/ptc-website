Research

Research & ideas.

For over 30 years, I have studied, educated, practiced and innovated around matters related to peace, conflict and justice through three interconnected tracks of work.
Three tracks of work

The questions driving the work.

Each track operates as its own research program — with field sites, labs, books, and applied tools — and each one feeds the next. Below: the lead question, what we study, and what has come out of it.

01
Theme One

Intractable Conflict.

Why do some conflicts resist every attempt at resolution — and what can complexity science reveal about how to shift them?

This research track explores:

  • Our Difficult Conversations Capture Lab, where we study the conditions for constructive encounters over moral conflicts.
  • Research on framing and motivation in community bridge-building over political and racial differences.
  • Applied work on organizational activism and leveraging multicultural conflict for institutional reform.
— Key outputs
  • The Five Percent — book
  • American Psychologist papers
  • Stanford Social Innovation Review
  • The Guardian feature on the Difficult Conversations Lab
Explore publications →
02
Theme Two

Conflict Intelligence.

A new scientific paradigm for engaging conflict optimally — moving beyond micro, short-term, linear models.

Standard approaches to conflict resolution rely on micro, short-term, linear models. This track works to expand the field to include more complex, holistic, dynamical-systems approaches.

  • Adaptive negotiation across power differences.
  • Adaptive mediation in extreme situations.
  • Adaptive cross-cultural conflict engagement.
— Key outputs
  • Making Conflict Work — book
  • CIQ Assessment (conflictintelligence.org)
  • CIQ App
  • HBR article on mediation
  • "Rethinking Intractable Conflict" — American Psychologist

03
Theme Three

Sustainable Peace.

What can the world's most peaceful societies teach us about building and sustaining peace?

This track includes:

  • Comparative studies of "peace systems" vs. "non-peace systems."
  • Ground-truthing field projects in Mauritius and Costa Rica.
  • The If Peace Could Speak initiative — applying machine learning and NLP to study "peace speech" across 20 nations ($1.2M partnership with Toyota Research Institute).
— Key outputs
  • Nature article
  • sustainingpeaceproject.com
  • Peace tech visualizations
  • AC4 research

Labs & Centers

Where the work happens.

Two centers anchor the research portfolio — bridging the science-practice nexus in conflict resolution and the transdisciplinary study of sustainable peace.

MD-ICCCR

Morton Deutsch International Center for Cooperation & Conflict Resolution.

Founded 1998 · Teachers College

Focused on the science-practice nexus in conflict resolution. Home of the doctoral program, the Science-Practice Blog, and decades of applied research in mediation, negotiation, and constructive controversy.

Visit ICCCR ↗
AC4

Advanced Consortium on Cooperation, Conflict & Complexity.

Founded 2008 · The Climate School

Transdisciplinary research on sustainably peaceful societies. Home of the Women, Peace and Security program, co-founded with Nobel Peace Prize laureate Leymah Gbowee.

Visit AC4 ↗
DCL

Difficult Conversations Capture Lab.

A flagship project of MD-ICCCR

Studies the conditions for constructive encounters over moral conflicts — bringing strangers across divides into a controlled setting and capturing the moments where things shift, or do not.
